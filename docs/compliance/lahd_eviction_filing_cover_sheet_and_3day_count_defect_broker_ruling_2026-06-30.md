## §2 Item 1 — LAHD Eviction Notice Filing Cover Sheet

### §2.1 What the cover sheet is

Per the operative LAHD instructions at [housing.lacity.gov/eviction-notices](https://housing.lacity.gov/eviction-notices) and the verbatim text on the cover sheet itself (Rev 2.6.2026):

> "This form is only required if you are submitting a physical copy of the eviction notice by mail or in person to the Los Angeles Housing Department (LAHD). It is not needed if you upload the eviction notice online."

The cover sheet is a **filing wrapper**, not a content requirement on the eviction notice. It accompanies a copy of the served notice when the landlord files via paper. It collects:

- Rental property info (owner name, address, unit, city, ZIP, APN, # of bedrooms)
- Tenant info (one tenant name; phone/email if known)
- Notice details (type checkbox: 3-Day / 10-Day / 30-Day / 60-Day / 90-Day / 120-Day / 6-Months / 1-Year; date served)
- Just-cause reason checkboxes (at-fault categories + no-fault categories with Landlord Declaration Case Number field)
- Tenant rent details (current monthly rent; total rent amount owed including non-noticed debt)
- Declaration under penalty of perjury (signed by Owner / Manager / Attorney / Other)

### §2.2 Statutory basis (verified)

The underlying filing obligation lives in:

- **LAMC § 151.09.C.9** (RSO units) — "A copy of any written notice terminating a tenancy shall be filed with the Department either electronically in the manner prescribed by the Department or by means of a form approved by the Department and the City Attorney for this purpose. The landlord shall file with the Department any such written notice terminating a tenancy within three business days of service on the tenant." Source: [LA City Clerk — Ordinance amending LAMC § 151.09 and § 165.05](https://cityclerk.lacity.org/onlinedocs/2023/23-0038_misc_4-21-25.pdf).
- **LAMC § 165.05.B.5** (JCO units) — identical "within three business days of service on the tenant" obligation, same paper-or-electronic election. Source: [AMLegal — LAMC § 165.05](https://codelibrary.amlegal.com/codes/los_angeles/latest/lamc/0-0-0-361771).

The cover sheet is the City-Attorney-approved form referenced in the "by means of a form approved by the Department and the City Attorney for this purpose" clause. Online portal at [lahd.service-now.com/efs](https://lahd.service-now.com/efs) is the alternative electronic path.

### §2.3 Affirmative-defense consequence if not filed

Per the cover sheet itself (page 2):

> "A tenant can raise an affirmative defense in an eviction for failure to provide a copy of the eviction notice to LAHD."

And per Law Office of David Piotrowski, [Notices to Terminate Tenancy Must Be Filed with LAHD](https://www.attorneydavid.com/blog/notices-to-terminate-tenancy-must-be-filed-with-lahd/) (citing LAMC § 165.05(B)(5)): "Failure to provide a copy of the notice of termination of tenancy to the LAHD serves as an affirmative defense that a tenant may raise to fight against the eviction. It can also be a misdemeanor."

The filing deadline is **3 business days from service on the tenant**, not 3 days. Business days exclude weekends (and conservatively, City of LA observed holidays). For the example notice served Tuesday June 30, 2026, the LAHD filing deadline is by end of business **Friday July 3, 2026** if July 3 is treated as a business day, or **Monday July 6, 2026** if Independence Day (observed) closes LAHD. **Broker policy call: treat LAHD-business-days as City-of-LA observed business days, not court days.** Filing by Monday July 6 is the conservative safe-harbor and aligns with City closure for the Independence Day observance.

### §2.4 What OwnerPilot needs to do (per §2.1 ruling)

The cover sheet is NOT a notice-face addition. It does not get attached to the served notice (the tenant does not receive the cover sheet — the cover sheet accompanies the landlord's filing to LAHD).

What OwnerPilot must produce alongside the served notice (and its companion RTC packet, registration certificate, and protections notice, all of which already are in the LA production flow):

1. **A post-service action checklist** for the owner, surfaced at notice-finalization time, including:
   - "File a copy of this notice with LAHD within 3 business days of service" (with deadline computed against City of LA observed business days)
   - "Preferred: upload via the online portal at [housing.lacity.gov/eviction-notices](https://housing.lacity.gov/eviction-notices) → Submit New Notice. Requires a free Angeleno Account."
   - "Alternative: mail a paper copy plus the LAHD Eviction Notice Filing Cover Sheet to LAHD Eviction Filing Section, PO BOX 17850, Los Angeles, CA 90057."
   - Link to the current cover sheet PDF (see §2.5 for URL discipline).

2. **A pre-filled cover sheet** as an optional artifact in the post-service packet. OwnerPilot already has every field the cover sheet asks for (owner name, property address, unit, city, ZIP, APN once Phase 2d address-resolution + parcel-lookup land, tenant first/last name, notice type = "3 Day", date served, just-cause reason = "Non-Payment of Rent", current monthly rent, total rent amount owed). The Declaration field stays unsigned because OwnerPilot is not the declarant.

3. **DO NOT** modify the served notice face to incorporate cover-sheet content. The notice and the cover sheet are two separate artifacts with two separate audiences (tenant receives the notice; LAHD receives the cover sheet + notice copy).

## §3 Item 2 — 3-Day Notice Day-Count Defect (CRITICAL)

### §3.1 The defect

The OwnerPilot-produced notice for 5537 La Mirada Ave Unit 202 (tenant Clifton Alexander, payee PTAG L LLC, total due $6,000.00) states verbatim:

> "The 3-day period to comply with this notice commences on June 30, 2026 and expires at the end of the day on July 2, 2026. The 3-day period excludes Saturdays, Sundays, and California judicial holidays."

The expiration date **July 2, 2026** is wrong. Correct expiration is **end of day Monday, July 6, 2026**.

### §3.2 Why — the day-count walk

Service date: **Tuesday, June 30, 2026** (per the notice's "Dated: June 29, 2026" face footer and the production timestamp suggesting same-day or next-day service).

Per CCP § 1161(2) as amended by AB 2343 (eff. 9/1/2019), the 3-day notice period **excludes Saturdays, Sundays, and other judicial holidays**. Per CCP § 12, the first day (day of service) is excluded; the last day is included. Source: [FindLaw — CCP § 1161](https://codes.findlaw.com/ca/code-of-civil-procedure/ccp-sect-1161/); [Legal Professionals, Inc. — AB 2343 day-count summary](https://www.legalprofessionalsinc.org/did-you-know-california-state-unlawful-detainer-rule-changed-effective-09-01-19/); [Hanson Bridgett — AB 2343](https://www.hansonbridgett.com/Publications/articles/2019-08-senior-care-new-ca-eviction-actions).

Walk forward from the day after service:

| Step | Calendar date | Day of week | Status | Days counted |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Day +1 | Wed Jul 1, 2026 | Wednesday | COUNT | 1 of 3 |
| Day +2 | Thu Jul 2, 2026 | Thursday | COUNT | 2 of 3 |
| Day +3 | Fri Jul 3, 2026 | Friday | **SKIP — CA judicial holiday** (Independence Day observed; Jul 4 = Sat) | 2 of 3 |
| Day +4 | Sat Jul 4, 2026 | Saturday | SKIP — weekend | 2 of 3 |
| Day +5 | Sun Jul 5, 2026 | Sunday | SKIP — weekend | 2 of 3 |
| Day +6 | Mon Jul 6, 2026 | Monday | COUNT | **3 of 3 — expiration** |

**Correct expiration: end of day Monday, July 6, 2026.**

### §3.3 Source — why Friday July 3, 2026 is a judicial holiday

July 4, 2026 falls on a Saturday. Per California Rule of Court 1.11 and CCP § 12a/§ 135, the preceding Friday (July 3, 2026) is the observed judicial holiday. This is confirmed by:

- [Los Angeles Superior Court — Holidays](https://www.lacourt.ca.gov/holiday/ui/index.aspx): "Friday, July 3, 2026, Independence Day"
- [Stanislaus Superior Court holiday schedule](https://www.stanislaus.courts.ca.gov/general-information/court-hours-holidays): "Independence Day | July 3 (Fri)" for 2026
- [TypeLaw — 2026 Court Holidays](https://www.typelaw.com/blog/2026-court-holidays-by-state-key-local-closures-that-affect-filing-deadline-calculations/): "Independence Day (observed) — Fri., July 3, 2026 (because July 4 is a Saturday)"
- [Central District of California bankruptcy court](https://www.cacb.uscourts.gov/sites/cacb/files/documents/news/PN%2025-023.pdf): "Friday, July 3 — Independence Day (Observed)"
- LA County observance rule (parallel): [lacounty.gov — Observed-Holidays rule](https://lacounty.gov/government/about-la-county/about/): "If January 1st, June 19th, July 4th, November 11th, December 25th falls upon a Saturday, the preceding Friday is a holiday."

The OwnerPilot holiday table (`holidays_la_city_dates_for_build_2026-06-19.md` §1.1) **already lists `2026-07-03` as a City of LA observed holiday for Independence Day (Sat → preceding Fri shift rule)**, and the broker-verified `CA_JUDICIAL_HOLIDAYS[2026]` table (referenced in the same doc §4) includes July 3, 2026 as a judicial holiday. The data is correct in the calendar; the defect is in the day-count engine's application of that calendar to this notice.

### §3.4 What this means — UD defeasibility

Per CCP § 1161(2), the 3-day period must elapse before an unlawful detainer can be filed. If the landlord files the UD on July 3, 2026 or later relying on the stated July 2 expiration — but the legally-correct expiration is July 6 — the UD is **premature and defeasible**. The tenant's affirmative defense is straightforward: the 3-day period was not yet expired when the UD was filed.

Per [Silverstein Eviction Law — 3-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Quit California](https://www.stevendsilverstein.com/eviction-help/notice-to-pay-rent-or-quit): "Do not file on Day 3 — wait until the notice has fully expired. Filing one day early means starting over."

Per [Avvo Q&A on 3-day notice period](https://www.avvo.com/legal-answers/what-is-the-notice-period-when-a-3-day-notice-to-p-6070117.html), citing CCP §§ 1161(2)–(3) and § 12: "Saturdays, Sundays, and other judicial holidays are not calculated as part of the three-day notice period."

Eshagian v. Cepeda (2025) requires the notice to include both the commencement date and expiration date on its face — and those dates must be legally correct. A facially-stated wrong expiration is itself a notice defect even before the UD-filing question.

### §3.5 Root-cause diagnosis (engineering)

The notice produced on or about June 29–30, 2026 ran the day-count engine and produced a 3-day expiration of July 2, 2026. That math treats July 1, 2026 + July 2, 2026 as the only two counted days and stops — which would only be correct if the engine were counting 2 days, not 3. Or, more likely, the engine **counted July 3 as a regular business day** and stopped on July 2 — which means either:

1. The judicial-holidays table the engine consulted at notice-production time did not include July 3, 2026 (a data defect — but the broker-verified `holidays_la_city.ts` and `CA_JUDICIAL_HOLIDAYS[2026]` both have it), OR
2. The day-count function failed to apply the judicial-holiday skip (a logic defect), OR
3. The engine consulted a stale or shadow holiday source.

**This is a [MUST FIX] critical-path engineering defect.** Recommend engineer:

1. Re-run the same notice through the production day-count engine using June 30, 2026 as the service date and capture the engine's output.
2. If output is still "July 2, 2026" → defect is in the engine (or its holiday source), not stale at this particular notice's production time. Open a Sev-1 ticket. Block notice production until fixed.
3. If output is now "July 6, 2026" → defect was data-staleness at notice-production time (e.g., `holidays_la_city.ts` not yet deployed at that moment). Confirm the holiday table is now live on production and add a deploy-time invariant check that asserts known shift dates (Jul 3, 2026 = Independence Day observed; Dec 24, 2027 = Christmas observed; Dec 31, 2027 = NYD-2028 cross-year shift).
4. Add a regression test: "given service date Tuesday June 30, 2026, expiration is Monday July 6, 2026" — bake this exact case into the day-count test suite so the defect is caught by CI from this day forward.

### §3.6 Immediate operator action

**DO NOT SERVE the current notice on Clifton Alexander.** It is facially defective.

1. Regenerate the notice with the correct expiration of **Monday, July 6, 2026** (end of day).
2. Re-date the notice for the actual service date (if service has not occurred). If the notice is being re-dated from June 29 → June 30, update the "Dated:" line accordingly and re-run the day-count from the new dated/service day forward.
3. Re-verify the regenerated notice's face: commencement date = day of service; expiration date = correctly-computed last counted day; "excludes Saturdays, Sundays, and California judicial holidays" disclaimer present.
4. Serve the corrected notice. Log the service date for the LAHD filing deadline computation.
5. File copy with LAHD within 3 business days per §2.

**If the notice was already served on June 30, 2026 with the wrong expiration on its face**, the affirmative-defense exposure is real but mitigable. Two paths:

- **Path A (safer):** Treat the defective notice as void. Serve a corrected notice the same day or next day. The original defective notice has no curative effect, but it has no statutory penalty for being served either — it just doesn't count. The corrected notice resets the clock.
- **Path B (riskier):** Wait until the legally-correct July 6, 2026 expiration before filing the UD, regardless of what the notice face said. This relies on the underlying statute, not the notice text, to define when the period expires. **However**, Eshagian v. Cepeda (2025) doctrine that commencement + expiration must appear on the notice face suggests the wrong face date is itself a notice defect, not just a service-timing question. Path A is the broker-recommended posture.
